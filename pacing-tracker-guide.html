Your spend tracking, explained simply.

This guide walks through how the Budget Pacing Tracker works — where the numbers come from, how the pacing calculation is done, and how to read and manage the dashboard day to day. It covers every Google Ads campaign across Electrical, Energy, Antennas, and Security.

1

What This Tool Does

The Budget Pacing Tracker is a live dashboard that shows, for every Google Ads campaign across all divisions, whether spend is on track against its budget for the current month — updated automatically every day, with no manual downloads required.

Think of it as an early-warning system: instead of finding out at the end of the month that a campaign massively overspent or barely spent at all, the dashboard flags it while there's still time to do something about it.

2

Where the Data Comes From

1

A small automated script runs daily

Each Google Ads account has a script installed that runs on a schedule (typically early morning), pulling each campaign's spend for the month automatically. No one needs to export, download, or upload anything.

2

It reports through yesterday, not "right now"

The script adds up spend through the end of the previous day — not including today, since today isn't finished yet. This keeps the pacing maths accurate rather than mixing in a partial, unfinished day.

3

It sends the numbers straight to the tracker

The figures are pushed automatically to the dashboard the moment the script finishes running — no waiting, no manual step in between.

💡

Because figures are reported through yesterday, the dashboard will always show something like "data as of day 6 of 31" — one day behind today's date. That's intentional, and keeps the numbers accurate rather than showing a half-finished day.

3

How the Pacing Maths Works

Each campaign has a Daily Budget set — the same figure that's actually configured in Google Ads for that campaign, not a monthly total. Everything else the tracker shows is worked out from that one number:

TermHow it's calculatedWhat it tells you
Monthly capDaily budget × 30.4What Google Ads itself won't exceed this month. 30.4 is Google's own average-days-per-month figure, used because calendar months vary from 28 to 31 days.
Expected spendDaily budget × days elapsed"Where this campaign should be" as of yesterday.
Pace %Actual spend ÷ expected spendHow the campaign compares to that plan.
Example: a campaign right on target
Spend so far (fill)Time elapsed (marker line)
💡

The tracker's pace bar shows this visually — the coloured fill is spend as a share of the monthly cap, and the vertical marker line shows how far through the month you are. If the fill passes the marker, the campaign is spending ahead of schedule.

⚠️

Because 30.4 is an average, campaigns will naturally read slightly "ahead" of pace late in a 31-day month, and slightly "behind" in a 28-day month, even with perfectly steady spend. That's expected drift, not a fault.

Managing Campaigns

1

Open Manage campaigns

Click the button in the top bar, then select the tab for the relevant division.

2

Add a campaign

Enter its name and daily budget at the bottom of the list, then click Add.

3

Change a budget

Edit the number directly in the list — it saves automatically.

4

Remove a campaign

Click the bin icon next to it. This also removes its spend history, so use with care.

💡

Budgets are stored per calendar month, so changing this month's budget won't affect how past months are shown.

7

Common Questions

A campaign shows "no data"
Either the automated script hasn't run yet for that campaign this month, or it's a brand-new campaign not yet picked up. Give it a day and check again.
A campaign has stopped updating
Check whether it's still Enabled in Google Ads. Paused or removed campaigns stop being reported, and the tracker will just keep showing their last known figures rather than resetting them to zero.
The numbers look a day behind
This is expected — see "Where the Data Comes From" above. The dashboard always reports through yesterday, never live same-day figures.
I'm signed in, but it says my email isn't on the access list
Access is restricted to an approved list of Microsoft/Entra sign-ins. Contact Phil Mumford to be added.
Why does a campaign at 103% pace on day 30 not seem urgent?
Late in a 31-day month, everything trends slightly "ahead of pace" by design, because the tracker's monthly cap uses Google's 30.4-day average rather than the real day count. A few percent over near month-end is normal drift, not a real problem.
