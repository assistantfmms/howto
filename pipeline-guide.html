This guide walks you through everything you need to know to keep the Jim's Energy sales pipeline up to date. The pipeline runs through Microsoft Planner — think of it like a job board where every card is a customer job moving from quote through to install.

1

How the Pipeline Works

Every job moves through six stages from left to right. As the job progresses, you drag the card to the next stage. The dashboard updates automatically overnight.

Adding a New Job

Always start from the template task — never create a blank new task. This keeps everything consistent and ensures the dashboard picks up all the right data.

1

Open Microsoft Planner

Go to planner.microsoft.com and open the "Jim's Electrical, Battery & Solar" board.

2

Find the "New Proposal" template task

Look for the task called "New Proposal" in the Proposals column. This is the master template — it's always there and never gets moved or deleted.

⚠️

Don't edit the "New Proposal" task itself. You need to copy it first, then edit the copy.

3

Copy the template task

Click the three dots (…) on the "New Proposal" card → select "Copy task". A new card will appear in the Proposals column.

Click the three dots on New Proposal, then select Copy task

4

Rename the task with the job address

Click the new card to open it. Change the title from "New Proposal" to the full job address — for example: 38 Stephen Tce, Norwood SA

💡

Always use the full street address including suburb and state. This is how the job appears on the dashboard.

5

Set the State label

Still inside the task card, find the Labels section. Select the state the job is in:

SAVicWANSWQld

Select the correct state label — one state only

🚨

Only select one state label. If you pick two states by mistake, the dashboard won't filter correctly.

6

Add a lead type label (if applicable)

If the job came in through a special lead source, add a second label alongside the state label:

LabelWhen to use it

If the job is a normal lead (not F&F or Referral), just leave this blank — only add the state label.

7

Fill in the Notes field

The Notes field is where all the financial data lives. It must be entered in exactly this format:

QUOTE: 21000, COST: 13500, MONTH: 2026-07, SYSTEM: 13.2kW Solar + 10kWh Battery

8

Save and close the task

Click anywhere outside the task card or press the X to close. The task saves automatically.

✅

The dashboard updates overnight. By tomorrow morning, your new job will appear on the dashboard with all its data.

3

The Notes Field — Getting It Right

The Notes field is the most important part. The system reads it automatically each night — if the format is wrong, the data won't appear on the dashboard.

FieldWhat it isExampleNotes
QUOTE:Total quoted price to customer (GST inclusive)21000Numbers only — no $, no commas
COST:Your cost for the job (GST inclusive)13500Numbers only — no $, no commas
MONTH:Expected install month2026-07Always YYYY-MM format (year dash month)
SYSTEM:System size and type13.2kW Solar + 10kWh BatteryFree text — describe it naturally

✅ Correct format:

QUOTE: 21000, COST: 13500, MONTH: 2026-07, SYSTEM: 13.2kW Solar + 10kWh Battery

❌ Incorrect formats (these will break the dashboard):

QUOTE: $21,000  ← No $ signs or commas in numbers
COST:13500  ← Need a space after the colon
MONTH: 07/2026  ← Must be YYYY-MM format
⚠️

Common mistakes: Don't add $ signs or commas to numbers. Don't use slashes for MONTH (07/2026 won't work — use 2026-07). Make sure there's a space after each colon.

Moving a Job Through the Pipeline

As a job progresses, drag the card to the next stage. This is what drives the pipeline weighting and forecast on the dashboard.

1

Drag the card to the new stage

In Planner's board view, drag the job card from its current column into the next one. The stage updates automatically.

2

Update the status label

Each time you move a job to a key stage, update its label to match. Open the task, go to Labels, and switch to the correct one:

Stage you're moving toLabel to apply
SentProp Sent
SignedProp Signed
Completed InstallsCompleted
💡

These labels show on the card in the board view so you can see at a glance where each job is up to without opening it.

3

Update the install month when it's locked in

When you move a job to Signed or Live Jobs, open the task and update the MONTH field in Notes if the install date has been confirmed or changed. Keeping this accurate is what makes the revenue forecast reliable.

4

Update the COST when you have final numbers

Once you have the final costs from your supplier or installer, update the COST value in Notes. This keeps the gross margin figure on the dashboard accurate.

5

Mark completed jobs

When a job is fully installed, drag it to Completed Installs. Don't delete it — completed jobs remain in the pipeline for reporting.

✅

Completed installs count as 100% committed revenue in your monthly totals.

Common Questions

I added a job but it's not showing on the dashboard
The dashboard updates overnight — your job will appear tomorrow morning. If it's still not there after a day, check that your Notes field is in the exact right format (see Section 3). The most common causes are missing a space after the colon, or using a $ sign in the QUOTE or COST fields.
A job's quote value is wrong on the dashboard
Open the task in Planner, go to the Notes field, and check the QUOTE value. Correct it and the dashboard will pick up the update overnight. Remember — numbers only, no $ sign, no comma (21000 not $21,000).
I moved a job to the wrong stage — how do I fix it?
Just drag it to the correct stage in Planner. The dashboard will update with the corrected stage overnight.
A job fell through — what do I do with the Planner card?
Move it to the Lost bucket in Planner. Don't delete it — keeping lost jobs helps track your close rate over time.
How do I access the dashboard?
The dashboard link is on your SharePoint site. You'll need to be signed in with your Microsoft account — if you're already signed into Microsoft 365, it should open automatically. If you see a login screen, sign in with your normal Microsoft password.
What's the difference between QUOTE and COST?
QUOTE is the total price you've quoted the customer (what they pay, including GST). COST is what it costs you to do the job — materials, labour, installer fees (also including GST). The dashboard uses these two numbers to calculate your gross margin automatically.
I don't know the cost yet — can I leave it blank?
Yes — just leave out the COST field or put 0. The job will still appear on the dashboard but the margin won't calculate until you add the real figure. Make sure to update it once you have it.
